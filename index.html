The Scent You Leave Behind

Your scent still clings to my shirt, so light—a quiet echo of our day, the warmth of arms wrapped soft and tight, refusing now to fade away.

I press the cloth against my face, and there you are, though not in sight—the comfort of your quiet grace, a hug that lingers through the night.

So every time I breathe you in, I smile, because I know it's true: this little world upon my skin means I spent the whole day with you.

Worry, thus Warmth

I’ve been holding this in my chest for a while now, because what happened that day meant more to me than I think you realized. It was our first real fight... well, not really, more so a small disagreement.

When I finally woke up and saw your messages, guilt hit me like a wave. I hated knowing I made you feel like I didn’t care when really, I care so much it aches sometimes. So I cried. Not because you yelled. You didn’t.

I cried because you didn’t become someone else in your frustration. Because you stayed you.

You didn’t turn cold. You didn’t mock me or twist the knife with silence. You listened. You stayed with me in the quiet after the apology, and instead of pushing me away, you laid me down beside you with this quiet tenderness that made my heart break and heal all at once.

Even in disappointment, you were still a safe place. Even in hurt, you didn’t let go. That night I felt something shift.

A few nights later, we found ourselves side by side again... you asked me what I pictured for our future. We imagined a small apartment filled with warm light. Wooden floors. Your mug always left half-full on the counter. My paints in the corner. We were honest. We were both scared. Still, through that worry, we stayed with the vision. Because we know it’s not just the destination that matters, but who we’re walking with.

So this is what I wanted to write down and keep. A reminder of how even in frustration, you showed me grace. And in fear, you gave me hope. Thank you for loving me the way you do.

That One Summer Night

It finally happened. The kind of missing that doesn’t just sit quietly but claws at your chest until it spills out in tears. I think I understand now—what you meant on the nights you said you missed me so badly it ached.

I was barely hanging on. My anxiety was through the roof. I was tired, overstimulated, deprived. And just when I’d usually want to crash into your arms after a long day, I couldn’t. I typed "I miss you" like I always did. Then I broke.

Tears started coming and they didn’t stop. I wanted you so badly in that moment, not just to talk to, but to hold me. To ground me. I just wanted you.

I cried because I missed my home.
And that’s you.

My Quiet Place

I said I felt like I can’t misstep,
that even silence shakes your ground,
that every pause becomes a weight
and I forget how to be found.

Sniffles broke my every line,
my breath in pieces, slow and rough.
But you stayed soft, you didn’t flinch
your gentleness was just enough.

And in that moment, grace arrived
not loud, just choosing me to stay.

Because you’re still that. don’t you see?
You’re still my home, my heart, my best friend.
And loving you through growing pains
is something I will never end.

So thank you for the way you stay,
for meeting fear with soft embrace.
You’ll always be my steady light,
my safest home, my quiet place.
