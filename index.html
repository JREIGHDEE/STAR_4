My head on your Chest

The first time I laid my head on your chest was the night I confessed. I remember thinking, Should I? Is this too much? But I did it anyway, and then I heard it. Your heart gave this quick, sudden thump, almost like it jumped. In my head I thought, Was that because of me? and I couldn’t stop smiling. I didn’t say anything, but in that moment I knew this was a place I wanted to come back to.

When we had our first kiss, I found myself doing it again without thinking. I pulled back, still catching my breath, and rested my head there. She’s going to feel how fast my heart’s going, I thought. Then I realized yours was just as quick, pounding under my ear. It made me want to stay there and just listen.

Then the first time we made love, afterwards, I stayed. My head on your bare chest, skin to skin. I didn’t even want to move. I remember thinking, This is it. This is the closest I’ve ever been to anyone. Your heart was slower now, steadier, and I felt like I could fall asleep right there and never wake up anywhere else.

Since then, it has become something I always do. In quiet moments, without really planning it, I will rest my head there again. Sometimes I close my eyes and think, What’s it saying this time? I notice the little changes, when it is quick, when it is calm. It feels like it tells me things you do not say out loud.

Somewhere along the way, it stopped being just a sound. It became something I need. Every time I hear it, I think about all those moments, the first startled thump, the rush after our kiss, the steady calm after our first night. And every time, I just want to stay there a little longer.

Saturday

I woke up alone today. The bed was still warm where you had been, but not warm enough. The air felt different without you there. I lay there for a few seconds, blinking at the ceiling, letting the quiet sink in. It wasn’t a bad quiet, but it wasn’t the kind I liked. I missed the sound of your breathing next to me. I missed the weight of you curled against me.

For a moment, I just stared at the empty space beside me and felt this little ache in my chest. I wondered where you had gone. Then I got up, and everything changed. When I stepped out of the bedroom, the smell hit me first. Something savory, warm, and familiar drifted through the air. Then I saw you, standing at the kitchen top, hair a little messy, moving with this gentle focus like the whole world outside that moment didn’t exist. The light from the window brushed against your face, and for a second, I just stood there quietly, not wanting to break it. Something in my chest softened right away. That ache from waking up alone was gone. I just… felt at peace seeing you there.

You turned when you noticed me and smiled. You asked me how I was, and I said I was fine. Then, almost casually, you told me you had already made coffee for me and left it on the nightstand before I woke up. I didn’t even notice it was there. That small detail hit me so hard. You thought of me first thing in the morning. You wanted to take care of me before I even opened my eyes.

I think that is what changed. Before, I loved the idea of our future. I dreamed of it and felt excited when I imagined it. But after that morning, I didn’t just love the idea anymore. I loved the certainty of it.

I walked closer, still smiling at you, and offered to help. You kept working on your beefloaf dish while I took over the eggs. The smell of both dishes mixing together made the kitchen feel warmer somehow. I don’t know why, but standing there beside you, cooking like it was the most natural thing in the world, made me feel… full. Not just in my stomach, but in my heart.

Right there at the table, while you were telling me something and smiling in that way you do, it hit me. We had already been feeling steady. But that morning, I saw it so clearly it was like watching a memory from the future. I could see you at the kitchen top, me coming up to hug you from behind, the warmth of the coffee you made, the easy rhythm of cooking side by side. The comfort of knowing this was ours.

And gods, I wanted it. Not just someday. I wanted it now. Since that day, we have felt different. Closer. More loving. It’s not just “I can see us together” anymore. It’s “I can’t imagine it any other way.”

Marked by You

The first bruise bloomed on my neck,
your lips spelling out a claim
in the quiet heat of our closeness.
It wasn’t just skin you touched—
it was the part of me that opens
only when trust is heavier than fear.

Since then,
you’ve painted me again,
and again—
your mouth a brush of hunger,
your hands anchoring me
to the world we build in whispers and gasps.

I have learned that loving you
is not just sweetness—
it’s the wild, unshaken rhythm
of wanting and being wanted.
It’s the way your eyes blaze,
and how my pulse forgets itself

when you’re riled and unafraid.
I let you see all of me,
even the shameless parts,
because you’ve shown me
how safety can be raw and breathless.

And every mark you leave
is a quiet promise—
that this fire between us
will not dim,
only deepen.

The First and After

The first time,
I led.
Not to claim, not to own,
but to show you the fire in me
and the way it bends toward you.

I wanted you stirred,
to see yourself
the way I do...strong, unshaken,
capable of making the world kneel.

And you did.

After that,
you began to lead.
Your touch spoke in a language
I never wanted to stop hearing.
You carried a quiet certainty,

and I followed,
not out of surrender,
but because I wanted to learn
every way you could love me.

Now each time,
I promise you something new.
A different way to pull you closer,
a different way to make you feel
how much of myself I’ve placed in your hands.

It is not a contest
of who holds more power.
It is a dance
where each step
is a gift we take turns giving.

And I will keep surprising you,
not just to keep the fire alive,
but because loving you
means never running out of ways
to make you know
you are worth every moment.

With love, always.

- Your Moon
