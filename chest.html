My head on your Chest

The first time I laid my head on your chest was the night I confessed. I remember thinking, Should I? Is this too much? But I did it anyway, and then I heard it. Your heart gave this quick, sudden thump, almost like it jumped. In my head I thought, Was that because of me? and I couldn’t stop smiling. I didn’t say anything, but in that moment I knew this was a place I wanted to come back to.

When we had our first kiss, I found myself doing it again without thinking. I pulled back, still catching my breath, and rested my head there. She’s going to feel how fast my heart’s going, I thought. Then I realized yours was just as quick, pounding under my ear. It made me want to stay there and just listen.

Then the first time we made love, afterwards, I stayed. My head on your bare chest, skin to skin. I didn’t even want to move. I remember thinking, This is it. This is the closest I’ve ever been to anyone. Your heart was slower now, steadier, and I felt like I could fall asleep right there and never wake up anywhere else.

Since then, it has become something I always do. In quiet moments, without really planning it, I will rest my head there again. Sometimes I close my eyes and think, What’s it saying this time? I notice the little changes, when it is quick, when it is calm. It feels like it tells me things you do not say out loud.

Somewhere along the way, it stopped being just a sound. It became something I need. Every time I hear it, I think about all those moments, the first startled thump, the rush after our kiss, the steady calm after our first night. And every time, I just want to stay there a little longer.
