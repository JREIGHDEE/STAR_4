Marked by You

The first bruise bloomed on my neck,
your lips spelling out a claim
in the quiet heat of our closeness.
It wasn’t just skin you touched—
it was the part of me that opens
only when trust is heavier than fear.

Since then,
you’ve painted me again,
and again—
your mouth a brush of hunger,
your hands anchoring me
to the world we build in whispers and gasps.

I have learned that loving you
is not just sweetness—
it’s the wild, unshaken rhythm
of wanting and being wanted.
It’s the way your eyes blaze,
and how my pulse forgets itself

when you’re riled and unafraid.
I let you see all of me,
even the shameless parts,
because you’ve shown me
how safety can be raw and breathless.

And every mark you leave
is a quiet promise—
that this fire between us
will not dim,
only deepen.
