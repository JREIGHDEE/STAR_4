The First and After

The first time,
I led.
Not to claim, not to own,
but to show you the fire in me
and the way it bends toward you.

I wanted you stirred,
to see yourself
the way I do...strong, unshaken,
capable of making the world kneel.

And you did.

After that,
you began to lead.
Your touch spoke in a language
I never wanted to stop hearing.
You carried a quiet certainty,

and I followed,
not out of surrender,
but because I wanted to learn
every way you could love me.

Now each time,
I promise you something new.
A different way to pull you closer,
a different way to make you feel
how much of myself Iâ€™ve placed in your hands.

It is not a contest
of who holds more power.
It is a dance
where each step
is a gift we take turns giving.

And I will keep surprising you,
not just to keep the fire alive,
but because loving you
means never running out of ways
to make you know
you are worth every moment.
